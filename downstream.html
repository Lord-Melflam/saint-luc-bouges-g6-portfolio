Downstream - Intermediate post-discharge solution

Transition pathway stream: faster room release while preserving patient continuity and care quality.

Downstream challenge statement

How might we organize an intermediate post-discharge solution so patients can safely leave inpatient rooms earlier, improving bed availability without degrading care continuity?

SCAMPER divergence (7 pillars from deliverable)

  • Substitute: waiting chairs instead of beds, family notification app, dedicated discharge receptionist, approved taxi option.
  • Combine: waiting area + cafeteria, lounge amenities, discharge linked with same-day follow-up appointments.
  • Adapt: airport-lounge model, Uber-like on-demand transport, click-and-collect slot logic, capsule-style waiting pods.
  • Modify: reminders to relatives, redefine discharge as leaving the room, involve relatives from admission, standard slots (9am/11am/2pm).
  • Put to another use: repurpose outpatient waiting rooms/off-peak spaces, use volunteers, create sheltered pickup area.
  • Eliminate: unnecessary departure admin friction, dependence on relatives as sole transport, uncertainty in departure timing.
  • Reverse: prepare discharge the day before, transport adapts to patient timing, hospital coordinates and notifies directly.

Converged direction (DFV-oriented)

The current concept combines three mutually reinforcing elements: (1) waiting-zone capacity, (2) digital coordination support, and (3) redesigned discharge process sequencing.

This cluster is retained because it is desirable for care teams and patients, feasible in staged deployment, and potentially viable through reduced congestion and better bed turnover.

Current status: in progress. Next step is pilot scenario definition with measurable operational indicators.
