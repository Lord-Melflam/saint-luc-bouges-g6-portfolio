Downstream challenge statement

How might we organize an intermediate post-discharge solution so patients can safely leave inpatient rooms earlier, improving bed availability without degrading care continuity?

SCAMPER ideation

  • Substitute: replace immediate stay in room by a transition/waiting area; or temporarily relocate patients who no longer need active care to a lower-pressure unit.
  • Combine: align discharge with same-day medical follow-up appointments so room release and patient pathway are synchronized.
  • Adapt: adapt concepts from airport waiting spaces to healthcare discharge transition zones.
  • Modify: move discharge timing earlier in the day whenever clinically possible.
  • Put to another use: convert underused hospital spaces into supervised discharge waiting areas.
  • Eliminate: remove low-value morning steps that delay departure (while preserving clinical safety).
  • Reverse: reorganize care sequence so tasks are better chained and discharge blockers are resolved earlier.

Converged direction (DFV-oriented)

The current concept combines three mutually reinforcing elements: (1) waiting-zone capacity, (2) digital coordination support, and (3) redesigned discharge process sequencing.

This cluster is retained because it is desirable for care teams and patients, feasible in staged deployment, and potentially viable through reduced congestion and better bed turnover.

Current status: in progress. Next step is pilot scenario definition with measurable operational indicators.
