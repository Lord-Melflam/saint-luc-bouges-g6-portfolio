During - Scheduling harmonization

During challenge statement

How might we harmonize scheduling practices across units through a shared and adaptable procedure, so that planning can still be completed smoothly even when one or more key staff members are absent?

Method used: structured brainstorming

The team used a structured brainstorming approach because scheduling problems come from multiple interconnected factors: different planning habits between units, replacement difficulty for key staff, and cross-unit coordination pressure in a complex hospital context.

Following the design-thinking approach, we first diverged (generate many ideas without early judgment), then converged (select realistic and coherent options).

Main ideas generated

  • Shared real-time planning board accessible to all relevant staff.
  • Standardized cross-unit planning process with clear rules and short explanatory videos.
  • Backup protocol for key roles when one or more staff members are absent.
  • Short inter-unit coordination meetings to anticipate absences and bed-pressure peaks.
  • Simple universal color-coding for priorities, urgent tasks, and absences.
  • Cross-training multiple members per unit on scheduling tasks.
  • Centralized scheduling information hub or shared dashboard.
  • Checklist of essential planning tasks to reduce omissions.
  • Predefined planning templates.

Idea clusters (harmonized synthesis)

Cluster Examples from brainstorming Intended effect
Shared visibility Real-time shared board, centralized planning hub/dashboard, color coding. Keep all units aligned despite absences or pressure peaks.
Process standardization Common planning structure, predefined templates, explanatory videos, checklist. Reduce variability between units and avoid recurring omissions.
Resilience to absence Backup protocol for key roles, cross-training, short inter-unit coordination meetings. Maintain continuity when one or more key actors are unavailable.

Evaluation and convergence

Ideas were assessed using DFV criteria (Desirable, Feasible, Viable). The retained direction combines: standardized templates, a shared adaptable procedure, clear backup rules, and improved cross-unit coordination.

Status: During stream is now completed at concept level and integrated into the group portfolio website.
