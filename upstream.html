Upstream - Forecast and communication

Early anticipation stream: predicting likely discharge windows and structuring coordinated communication.

Overview Summary Weeks Annexes Upstream During Downstream Deliverables References

Upstream objective

Reduce discharge uncertainty before the final day by combining a practical forecast logic with standardized team/patient communication.

Challenge statement

How might we better predict length of stay and improve communication with patients about their care pathway, to anticipate discharge earlier and more accurately?

Why this ideation method

Following the W7 ideation framework, we used a morphological matrix to diverge then converge in a structured way. This method generated realistic combinations quickly and matched the systems-oriented nature of the problem.

  • Inputs for anticipation: historical LOS, diagnosis/procedure type, social discharge constraints.
  • Prediction approach: simple rules, risk score, advanced ML.
  • Communication channel: nurse script, printed pathway sheet, SMS reminder.
  • Update timing: admission, D-2, D-1, discharge day.

Operational logic

D-2: initial expected discharge window shared in team board + first patient/family communication.
D-1: confidence update, unresolved blockers flagged, responsibility assignment.
D0: final confirmation workflow with explicit fallback if delay occurs.

Why the selected concept is robust

  • Desirable: clearer visibility for teams and better transparency for patients/families.
  • Feasible: low technical burden and direct compatibility with current operations.
  • Viable: controlled implementation cost and staged scaling by unit.

Expected impact KPIs: better discharge-date accuracy, more patients informed 24h+ in advance, fewer last-minute delays.
