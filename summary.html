Project objective

Build a coherent discharge optimization strategy that is operationally realistic and progressively testable in hospital conditions. The project combines design thinking, field feedback, and iterative prototyping to reduce bed-flow friction.

Problem framing and strategic split

The initial challenge was too broad to be solved as a single intervention. We split it into three synchronized streams: Upstream (anticipation and communication), During (planning harmonization), and Downstream (post-discharge transition). This made ownership clear and enabled parallel convergence.

Timeline synthesis

W1-W3: methodology foundations, empathizing strategy, stakeholder mapping.
W4-W7: problem definition, ideation, BMC, intermediate portfolio outputs.
W9-W10: finance/regulation inputs and three-stream decomposition.
W11: stream-level convergence, portfolio iteration 2, and prototype consolidation.

Stream status and outcomes

  • Upstream (completed): DFV-converged concept plus runnable prototype stack (forecast + board + communication).
  • During (completed): standardized scheduling framework with backup protocol and cross-unit coordination logic.
  • Downstream (in progress): SCAMPER divergence and DFV-selected cluster around waiting-zone + digital coordination + process rules.
  • Lheo integration stream (in progress): cross-stream consolidation and final integration support.
